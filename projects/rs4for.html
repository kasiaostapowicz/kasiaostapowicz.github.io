RS4FOR
Project

Forest Change Detection and Monitoring
Using Passive and Active Remote Sensing Data

2016 – 2019 Principal Investigator National Science Centre Poland Polish Carpathians

Background

Forest ecosystems under pressure

The RS4FOR project addressed environmental monitoring of forest ecosystems in the Polish Carpathians — a region exemplifying temperate forests in mountainous terrain. By integrating passive and active remote sensing data, the project enhanced the accuracy and efficiency of detecting and monitoring changes in forest cover.

Objectives

Key research aims

  • 01 Forest cover dynamics — detection of conversions and modifications using multi-decadal Earth Observation datasets (1978–2020)
  • 02 Predictive modelling — robust models predicting changes in forest structure to support proactive management
  • 03 Forest health assessment — advanced diagnostics enabling timely intervention to mitigate disease and degradation
  • 04 Old-growth forest conservation — insights into the status and dynamics of old-growth stands for conservation planning

Trajectories, Causes and Consequences
of Land Cover and Land Use Changes in Central Europe

TRACE Project  ·  2019–2023
National Science Centre Poland  ·  Grant no. 2018/29/B/ST10/02979

Land Cover Change Remote Sensing Time Series Biodiversity Carbon Dynamics Central Europe

Background

Land system change in post-socialist Europe

The TRACE project investigated land cover and land use change trajectories across Central Europe (Czechia, Hungary, Poland, Slovakia) over five decades, focusing on the profound restructuring of land systems following the 1990 political transition from centralised to market economies and the 2004 EU accession. By combining optical and radar satellite time series with socio-economic datasets, the project sought to disentangle the biophysical signals of agricultural abandonment, urban expansion, and wildland-urban interface dynamics from the institutional and economic drivers that produced them, and to quantify downstream consequences for biodiversity and carbon pools.

Objectives

Research objectives

  • 01 Land cover change mapping — multi-decadal remote sensing analysis of agricultural abandonment and urban expansion across Central Europe
  • 02 Driver analysis — ensemble modelling and telecoupling framework to identify socio-economic, political, and institutional causes of land change
  • 03 Biodiversity assessment — remote sensing-based indicators of biodiversity change including roadless area mapping at regional scale
  • 04 Carbon dynamics — quantification of land change impacts on carbon pools and fluxes across the study region

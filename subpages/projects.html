Research projects

Principal Investigator (PI)

As Principal Investigator or a key contributing researcher, I have been actively engaged in a wide range of research initiatives aimed at understanding and monitoring environmental change, with a particular focus on land use and land cover dynamics within the framework of land system science. My work integrates remote sensing, geospatial analysis, and advanced methods such as artificial intelligence and machine learning to investigate the trajectories, drivers, and impacts of these changes across various spatial and temporal scales. A core aspect of my research involves the analysis of long-term environmental change, spanning the past 150 years, while also developing projections of future land cover scenarios. These efforts are grounded in specific geographic contexts, including Central Europe, mountainous regions, and fragile polar ecosystems. By utilizing both passive and active remote sensing technologies, my research addresses critical challenges such as vegetation health, phenology, and biodiversity loss, providing valuable insights into human-environment interactions and their consequences for sustainability.
